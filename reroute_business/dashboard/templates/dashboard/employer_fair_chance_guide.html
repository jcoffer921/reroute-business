Fair-Chance Hiring Guide

Fair-chance hiring is not just the right thing to do. This guide will help you build equitable, effective hiring practices.

What is Fair-Chance Hiring?

Fair-chance hiring means evaluating job candidates based on their skills, qualifications, and potential before considering criminal history. It is about giving people an equitable opportunity to be assessed for who they are today.

Legal Framework

Many states and cities have "Ban the Box" laws that prohibit asking about criminal history on initial job applications. Even where not legally required, fair-chance practices are increasingly recognized as best practice for responsible employers.

Best Practices for Screening

  • Remove conviction history questions from initial applications
  • Evaluate candidates on skills and qualifications first
  • Conduct background checks only after a conditional offer
  • Consider the nature and age of any offense relative to the job
  • Give candidates a chance to explain and provide context
  • Document your evaluation process for consistency

The Business Case

  • Access a wider talent pool of motivated candidates
  • Higher retention rates as fair-chance hires often stay longer
  • Positive brand reputation and community impact
  • Tax credits and incentives in many jurisdictions
  • Reduced hiring costs through loyalty and engagement

Creating an Inclusive Workplace

Fair-chance hiring is most effective when paired with supportive onboarding. Consider mentorship programs, clear expectations, and a culture that values growth and second chances. Your existing team benefits from diversity of experience.
